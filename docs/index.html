Why these numbers are accurate

Every governing input traces to a real NASA dataset or a closed-form calculation. Only the relay revisit cadence is TBR, pending the Phase A link budget.

Earth-visibility geometry (drives blackout) LOLA polar maps averaged over the 18.6-yr lunar nutation cycle; NASA PGDA publishes an explicit Earth Visibility product for the south pole.
Blackout duration used in model ~14 days per ~29.5-day month (NASA VIPER Lunar Operations).
Continuously sunlit rim reference A site near Shackleton's rim is sunlit ~240 days/yr (LOLA) — the DTE-capable high point.
PSR floor (no Sun, no Earth) 0% averaged illumination; Diviner shows sunlit rim ~300 K vs cryogenic PSR floors.
Light time 384,400 km / c = 1.28 s one-way (fixed).
Relay revisit (TBR) Kepler III for a 100 km low orbiter → period ≈ 1.96 h; deployed LCRNS cadence pending Phase A.
Sources: NASA — Illumination Map of the Moon's South Pole (LROC) · NASA PGDA — Lunar Polar Illumination (incl. Earth Visibility), 240–60 m/px · NASA VIPER Lunar Operations · NASA SCaN — LCRNS lunar relays. Model + 26 tests: github.com/aryanputta/lunarlinkbench.
